Web Platform discrepancies

"<!--" is a line comment (same as "//").

In JScript, the following are ignored if they are at the end of the script (i.e., just followed by whitespace lines):

Either:

  1. A line that starts with just whitespace and comments and consists of "-->" followed by anything except new lines and finally followed by another "-->"; or
  2. one or two lines that start with just whitespace and comments and consist of just "-->".

"-->" at the end of the last line of the script causes the line to be ignored unless the "-->" occurs within a comment. The last non-whitespace line of the script is ignored if it ends with "-->" and doesn't contain "//" or "<!--" (but not in eval).

In SpiderMonkey, "-->" on any line that starts with just whitespace and comments is treated as a line comment (same as //). (Also, ;version=1.6, 1.7 or 1.8 or 1;e4x=1 enables E4X "<!-- -->" comments.)

In Carakan, Nitro and V8, "-->" on any line that starts with just whitespace (but not comments) is treated as a line comment (same as "//").

See also http://wiki.whatwg.org/wiki/Web_ECMAScript#HTML_comments
